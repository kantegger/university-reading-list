## Citation

Levy, J. M., Hirt, S. A., & Dawkins, C. J. (2024). _Contemporary Urban Planning_ (12th ed.). Routledge.

## Knowledge and Historical Context

Urban geography emerged as cities became the dominant form of human settlement in the 20th century. The field developed from urban sociology and economic geography, incorporating insights from planning, architecture, and public policy to understand urban processes and patterns.

This textbook represents contemporary urban geography's engagement with 21st-century urban challenges including globalization, technological change, environmental sustainability, and social equity. It reflects the field's evolution from descriptive urban studies to analytical frameworks for understanding urban systems and planning.

## Educational Philosophy

The textbook emphasizes that urban geography is essential for understanding how the majority of humanity lives and works. It teaches students to analyze urban systems spatially while developing critical thinking about urban planning, policy, and the social and environmental impacts of urbanization.

## Core Concepts

### Urban Systems and Hierarchy

City systems, urban hierarchy, central place theory, and the role of cities in regional and global economic networks.

### Urbanization Processes

Historical and contemporary patterns of urbanization, suburbanization, counter-urbanization, and re-urbanization in different world regions.

### Urban Structure and Land Use

Models of urban structure, land use patterns, residential segregation, and the spatial organization of urban activities.

### Urban Economics and Development

Urban economic base, agglomeration economies, urban real estate markets, and economic development strategies.

### Transportation and Mobility

Urban transportation systems, accessibility, traffic patterns, public transit, and sustainable mobility solutions.

### Housing and Neighborhoods

Housing markets, residential patterns, neighborhood change, gentrification, and housing policy.

### Urban Planning and Design

Planning theory and practice, zoning, urban design principles, and the role of planning in shaping urban form.

### Urban Sustainability and Environment

Urban ecology, sustainable development, green infrastructure, climate change adaptation, and environmental justice.

## Significance and Impact

This textbook has influenced urban geography education by integrating planning perspectives with geographic analysis of urban processes. Its practical approach has helped establish urban geography as a field relevant to urban planning, policy-making, and urban development practice.

The book's attention to contemporary urban challenges has shaped how urban geography engages with issues of sustainability, technology, and social equity in urban environments.

## Conclusion

Contemporary Urban Planning provides essential knowledge for understanding cities as complex spatial systems and the planning processes that shape urban development. Its integration of geographic analysis with planning practice prepares students for careers in urban planning, economic development, and urban policy.

The textbook's comprehensive coverage of urban processes and contemporary challenges makes it valuable for students pursuing graduate study in urban studies, planning, or public policy, as well as professionals working in urban development and management.
