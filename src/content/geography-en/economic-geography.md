## Citation

Coe, N. M., Kelly, P. F., & Yeung, H. W. (2024). _Economic Geography: A Contemporary Introduction_ (4th ed.). Sage Publications.

## Knowledge and Historical Context

Economic geography emerged in the early 20th century from commercial geography and location theory, developing through various theoretical phases including spatial analysis, behavioral approaches, and contemporary relational and institutional perspectives. The field has evolved to address globalization, technological change, and new forms of economic organization.

This textbook represents contemporary economic geography's engagement with global economic restructuring, financialization, and digital transformation. It reflects the field's evolution from descriptive industrial geography to sophisticated analysis of economic networks, innovation systems, and uneven development.

## Educational Philosophy

The textbook emphasizes that economic activities are inherently spatial and that understanding economic processes requires geographic analysis. It teaches students to examine economic phenomena across multiple scales while developing critical thinking about globalization, development, and economic inequality.

## Core Concepts

### Introduction to Economic Geography

Theoretical foundations, key concepts, and methodological approaches in economic geography, including the relationship between economy and space.

### Globalization and Economic Networks

Global production networks, value chains, multinational corporations, and the spatial organization of global economic activities.

### Location Theory and Industrial Geography

Classical and contemporary location theory, industrial clustering, agglomeration economies, and factors influencing industrial location decisions.

### Innovation and Knowledge Economy

Geography of innovation, knowledge spillovers, creative industries, and the spatial dimensions of technological change and entrepreneurship.

### Labor Markets and Employment

Geographic labor markets, migration, skills, unemployment, and the spatial dimensions of work and employment relations.

### Financial Geography

Global financial centers, financial flows, banking networks, and the geographic dimensions of finance and investment.

### Development and Underdevelopment

Theories of regional development, core-periphery relationships, development strategies, and the geography of global inequality.

### Consumption and Retail Geography

Geographies of consumption, retail location, shopping behavior, and the spatial organization of commercial activities.

## Significance and Impact

This textbook has shaped economic geography education by providing comprehensive coverage of both traditional and contemporary approaches to understanding economic space. Its integration of global and local perspectives has influenced how economic geography engages with globalization and development issues.

The book's attention to empirical examples and case studies has helped establish economic geography as a field relevant to economic development, business strategy, and policy-making.

## Conclusion

Economic Geography provides essential knowledge for understanding the spatial dimensions of economic activity in an increasingly globalized world. Its comprehensive approach to economic processes and spatial analysis prepares students for careers in economic development, business consulting, and economic policy.

The textbook's integration of theoretical rigor with practical applications makes it valuable for students pursuing graduate study in economic geography, development studies, or business, as well as professionals working in economic development and regional planning.
